One of cricket's modern greats

About M.S.Dhoni

MS Dhoni, born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, India, is a former Indian cricketer and captain renowned for his exceptional wicket-keeping and batting skills.

He captained India to victories in the 2007 ICC T20 World Cup, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy. Known for his calm demeanor and strategic acumen, Dhoni is celebrated as one of cricket's greatest leaders.

He retired from international cricket in August 2020 but continues to play in the IPL with the Chennai Super Kings, where he remains a key player and captain.

This tribute page celebrates his achievements and contributions to the sport of cricket.

M.S.Dhoni's cricketing achievements are distinguished and impactful:

  • ICC T20 World Cup 2007: Led India to victory in the inaugural T20 World Cup.
  • ICC Cricket World Cup 2011: Captained India to win the World Cup, playing a crucial role in the final.
  • ICC Champions Trophy 2013: Guided India to victory in the Champions Trophy.
  • Number 1 Test Ranking: Achieved and maintained the top spot in the ICC Test Team Rankings.
  • Indian Premier League (IPL): Won multiple IPL titles with the Chennai Super Kings (CSK).
